# MOD-003 — Collecting and Storing Evidence

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> A punishment record should be understandable without relying on a staff member’s memory.

## 1. Purpose

Collect and store evidence in a way that is clear, fair, and safe for player privacy.

## 2. Scope

Evidence related to player reports, punishments, appeals, exploits, staff misconduct, security incidents, compensation, and operational failures.

## 3. Who does what

- **Case owner:** Collects, labels, and protects relevant evidence.
- **Administrators:** Access sensitive server/platform logs.
- **Staff Manager:** Controls staff misconduct records.
- **Founders:** Approve disclosure outside normal internal review.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Evidence priority
Prefer, in order:
1. Server or platform logs with timestamps and identifiers.
2. Direct message links, audit logs, console output, database records, or code repository history.
3. Full screenshots/video showing context.
4. Consistent witness statements.
5. Cropped or second-hand material, which should be corroborated.

### 4.2 Collection
1. Record source, collector, date/time, and the issue it supports.
2. Preserve original files when practical; work from copies for annotations.
3. Capture enough preceding and following context to avoid misleading excerpts.
4. Record timezone and convert to a common reference when building a timeline.
5. For in-game locations, include world and coordinates only when needed and protect hidden/unreleased locations.

### 4.3 Integrity
Do not edit originals, fabricate reenactments as real evidence, or delete inconvenient material. Annotations should be placed in a separate copy and clearly labeled. If a screenshot may be altered, seek logs or another source.

### 4.4 Privacy minimization
Collect only what is relevant. Redact unrelated email addresses, IP addresses, payment details, private conversations, and third-party personal information before broader staff sharing. Never post sensitive evidence publicly merely to justify a decision.

### 4.5 Storage and storage
- Routine moderation: retain while punishment is active and for at least 12 months after closure.
- Permanent bans, major exploits, staff misconduct, payment disputes, and security incidents: retain longer as approved by a Founder.
- Duplicate or irrelevant evidence: securely remove when no longer needed.
- Keep records in approved staff storage with access limited by role.

### 4.6 Evidence requests
Players may receive a plain-language explanation and relevant rule, but not internal logs, private reports, security methods, personal data of others, or information that helps evade detection. Appeals are reviewed from the record rather than by public evidence debate.

## 5. When to get senior help

Get senior help when evidence contains doxxing, payment information, private staff data, security secrets, illegal material, or credible real-world threats. Do not download or redistribute illegal content beyond what is necessary for reporting and safety.

## 6. What to record

- Evidence inventory
- Original source and timestamp
- Redaction notes
- Access history where available
- Storage or deletion date

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Source and collector recorded
- [ ] Original preserved
- [ ] Context included
- [ ] Timeline timezone normalized
- [ ] Unrelated personal information minimized
- [ ] Access restricted appropriately
- [ ] Storage period assigned
