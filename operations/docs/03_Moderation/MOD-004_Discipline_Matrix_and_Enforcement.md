# MOD-004 — Punishment Guide and Enforcement

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Use fair punishments that match the harm. Similar cases should receive similar treatment. Senior staff may review unusual cases or important details.

## 2. Scope

Warnings, mutes, kicks, temporary bans, permanent bans, content removal, repair or repayment, restrictions, and related enforcement across Eldenstead services.

## 3. Who does what

- **Moderator:** May issue warnings, mutes, kicks, and temporary bans up to 7 days when supported by evidence.
- **Administrator:** May issue longer temporary bans and emergency permanent bans subject to prompt review.
- **Staff Manager/Founder:** Approves normal permanent bans, major exceptions, and staff-related enforcement.
- **Appeal reviewer:** Must not be the sole person who issued the original action.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Decide how serious the case is
Consider intent, harm, repetition, targeting, scale, concealment, cooperation, prior relevant history, and whether the player exploited staff trust or technical access. Do not increase punishment because a player criticized staff respectfully.

### 4.2 Suggested starting punishments

| Category | First minor offense | Repeated/moderate | Severe or malicious |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spam, excessive caps, disruption | Reminder/warning | 15 min–24 hr mute | 1–7 day ban for sustained evasion/raid behavior |
| Insults or non-severe harassment | Warning | 1–24 hr mute | 1–7 day ban if targeted/repeated |
| Hate speech, severe harassment, threats | 1–7 day ban | 7–30 day ban | Permanent ban recommendation depending on threat and context |
| Grief/theft outside allowed gameplay | Restore + warning | 1–14 day ban + repair or repayment | 30 day/permanent for destructive organized abuse |
| Cheating or prohibited client use | Evidence review | 7–30 day ban | Permanent for repeated, distributed, or destructive cheating |
| Exploit/dupe abuse | Contain + investigate | 14–30 day ban + rollback | Permanent for major economy/server harm or distribution |
| Ban/mute evasion | Extend original action | 7–30 day additional action | Permanent for persistent organized evasion |
| Doxxing, credible real-world threats, sexual exploitation | Immediate containment | Senior review | Permanent ban and platform reporting where appropriate |
| Staff impersonation/fraud | Content removal + restriction | 7–30 day ban | Permanent for theft, account theft or login fraud, or repeated abuse |

This table is a starting point, not an automatic answer. Write down why the final punishment is fair and matches the harm.

### 4.3 Steps for giving a punishment
1. Verify player identity and relevant rule.
2. Confirm evidence and authority.
3. Record the action before or immediately after issuing it.
4. Use a clear reason that is factual and safe for the player to see.
5. State duration and appeal route.
6. Apply related actions only when necessary, such as content removal, rollback, economy correction, or access restriction.

### 4.4 When several rules were broken
Do not give an extreme punishment just because several rules were broken. Focus on the main harmful action, note the related behavior, and choose one fair result.

### 4.5 When a punishment covers other platforms
Cross-platform bans should be used when the conduct affects community safety, involves evasion, or presents an ongoing risk. A minor in-game violation should not automatically erase unrelated website access.

### 4.6 Old and finished punishments
Keep finished punishments in the private record so staff can check for repeated behavior. Do not show them publicly to shame a player. Old minor cases should matter less than recent cases of the same kind.

## 5. When to get senior help

Permanent bans, payment fraud, doxxing, credible threats, exploitation involving staff access, or actions outside this matrix require senior approval. Emergency permanent bans may be used to stop immediate harm but must be reviewed promptly.

## 6. What to record

- Player identifiers
- Rule and evidence
- Prior relevant history
- Action, duration, issuer, and approver
- Related rollback/repair or repayment
- Notification and appeal link

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Identity and rule verified
- [ ] Relevant evidence reviewed
- [ ] Conflict of interest checked
- [ ] Severity factors considered
- [ ] Authority and approval confirmed
- [ ] Reason and duration recorded
- [ ] Appeal route provided
- [ ] Related restoration/restriction completed
