# MOD-007 — Reports About Staff Misconduct

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Handle reports about staff members safely, fairly, and without retaliation.

## 2. Scope

Reports of abuse of authority, leaks, harassment, favoritism, retaliation, evidence manipulation, conflicts of interest, login detail misuse, fraud, or other staff-policy violations.

## 3. Who does what

- **Recipient:** Preserves the report and routes it away from the accused.
- **Staff Manager:** Leads ordinary investigations.
- **Founder:** Leads cases involving senior staff, security, money, or potential removal.
- **Independent reviewer:** Used where the normal chain has a conflict.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Intake and protection
1. Create a restricted case visible only to necessary reviewers.
2. Capture the allegation, dates, systems, witnesses, and evidence.
3. Ask whether retaliation or ongoing access creates immediate risk.
4. Do not notify the accused until evidence and access risk are considered.

### 4.2 Interim measures
Temporary measures may include removing a case assignment, pausing moderation authority, restricting production access, or placing the person on administrative leave. State internally that interim action is not a final finding.

### 4.3 Investigation
1. Assign a reviewer with no close relationship or dispute involving the parties.
2. Preserve audit logs, messages, access records, and relevant case history.
3. Interview the reporter, witnesses, and accused separately.
4. Give the accused a fair summary and opportunity to respond, except where safety or legal constraints require delay.
5. Determine findings for each allegation: substantiated, unsubstantiated, inconclusive, or policy concern without misconduct.

### 4.4 Outcomes
Possible outcomes include coaching, retraining, written warning, conflict reassignment, access reduction, probation, suspension, removal, security response, or no action. The outcome should match evidence, harm, intent, pattern, and role responsibility.

### 4.5 Anti-retaliation
Retaliation includes punishment, exclusion, threats, rumor-spreading, doxxing, schedule manipulation, or misuse of private data because someone reported or participated in a review. Treat retaliation as a separate serious allegation.

### 4.6 Communication
Notify the reporter that the matter was reviewed, while limiting private employment/staff details. Notify the accused of the finding and requirements. Public statements should be rare, factual, and approved by a Founder.

## 5. When to get senior help

Reports involving a Founder should be handled by the other Founder or an independent trusted reviewer where available. Login Detail misuse, payment issues, threats, doxxing, or evidence destruction require immediate Founder/security escalation.

## 6. What to record

- Restricted case record
- Conflict assessment
- Interim measures
- Evidence inventory and interviews
- Findings per allegation
- Outcome and approver
- Anti-retaliation follow-up

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Case restricted from the accused
- [ ] Retaliation/access risk assessed
- [ ] Independent reviewer assigned
- [ ] Evidence preserved
- [ ] All parties heard where safe
- [ ] Findings separated by allegation
- [ ] Outcome approved and documented
- [ ] Follow-up checks completed
