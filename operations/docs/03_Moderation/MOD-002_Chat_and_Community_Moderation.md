# MOD-002 — Moderating Chat and Community Spaces

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Keep chat and community spaces safe while allowing normal discussion and roleplay.

## 2. Scope

Public and semi-public Eldenstead communication, including messages, usernames, signs, books, builds used as communication, voice channels, forum posts, and linked media.

## 3. Who does what

- **Helpers:** De-escalate, redirect, and collect evidence.
- **Moderators:** Enforce communication rules and issue fair actions that match the harm.
- **Administrators:** Handle severe threats, raids, coordinated harassment, and cross-platform cases.
- **Forum/Discord specialists:** Apply platform tools while keeping one case record.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Assess context
Determine where the message appeared, who could see it, whether it was roleplay, whether participants consented, whether it targeted a person/group, and whether it continued after a boundary was set. Roleplay is not a defense for harassment, slurs, threats, sexual content involving minors, or evasion of rules.

### 4.2 Use progressive intervention
For low-level disruption:
1. Calm public reminder or private redirection.
2. Formal warning if behavior continues or the rule is clear.
3. Slowmode, channel restriction, mute, or removal when needed.
4. Ban for severe or repeated conduct according to MOD-004.

Skip early steps for serious threats, doxxing, hate speech, sexual exploitation, malicious raids, or clear ban evasion.

### 4.3 De-escalation language
- State the behavior, not an insult about the person.
- Give one clear instruction.
- Avoid arguing about the entire rulebook in public.
- Move detailed discussion to a ticket or appeal.
- Do not compete with other staff in the same channel; one moderator leads.

### 4.4 Spam and raids
1. Enable slowmode or temporary channel lockdown.
2. Restrict new or suspicious accounts using available platform tools.
3. Preserve sample messages, invite sources, account IDs, and timestamps.
4. Remove malicious content in batches after evidence is captured.
5. Post one calm update; do not repeatedly amplify raid content.

### 4.5 Names, skins, builds, books, and signs
Treat intentionally offensive or impersonating content as communication. Ask for a change when safe; use removal or punishment when the content is severe, repeated, or designed to evade filters.

### 4.6 Staff presence
Staff should not bait users into violations, join pile-ons, or use sarcasm while issuing an official direction. When playing normally, identify when a message becomes an official moderation instruction.

## 5. When to get senior help

Get senior help at once for credible real-world threats, doxxing, sexual content involving minors, extortion, coordinated raids, impersonation of staff for fraud, or conduct involving a staff member.

## 6. What to record

- Message links or log excerpts
- Screenshots with context
- Platform and channel
- Staff directions given
- Action and duration
- Related accounts or raid indicators

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Context and audience reviewed
- [ ] Evidence captured before deletion where safe
- [ ] One moderator leads the interaction
- [ ] Clear instruction given
- [ ] Action follows MOD-004
- [ ] Cross-platform behavior linked in one case
- [ ] Severe safety issues escalated
