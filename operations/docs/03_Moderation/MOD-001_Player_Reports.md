# MOD-001 — Handling Player Reports

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Handle player reports fairly, protect evidence, and send serious cases to the right staff member.

## 2. Scope

Reports submitted in-game, through Discord tickets, website forms, forums, direct messages redirected into official channels, or staff observation.

## 3. Who does what

- **Helper/Support:** Receives the report, checks completeness, and routes it.
- **Moderator:** Investigates routine cases and applies authorized outcomes.
- **Administrator:** Handles severe, complex, cross-platform, or disputed cases.
- **Staff Manager:** Reviews staff-related or conflict-of-interest cases.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Intake
Create one case record and capture:
- Reporter and reported player identifiers
- Approximate date/time and timezone
- Server/world/location/channel
- Description of what happened
- Witnesses
- Screenshots, video, logs, or message links
- Whether the behavior is ongoing or presents immediate safety risk

Acknowledge the report without promising a punishment or revealing private outcomes.

### 4.2 Immediate safety check
If harassment, threats, doxxing, account compromise, destructive grief, active cheating, or an exploit is ongoing, contain the immediate harm using the least disruptive authorized action. Preserve evidence first when possible.

### 4.3 Conflict check
The investigator must not be directly involved, closely aligned with either party, or reviewing their own earlier action. Reassign if needed.

### 4.4 Investigation
1. Build a timeline from independent sources.
2. Verify that screenshots are complete enough to identify context; obtain message links or server logs when possible.
3. Review relevant chat, command, anticheat, inventory, economy, claim, and access logs.
4. Interview witnesses separately when needed.
5. Invite the reported player to provide context unless doing so could risk evidence or safety.
6. Apply the standard of **more likely than not** for normal moderation; use stronger corroboration for permanent or highly consequential actions.

### 4.5 Decision
Choose: no violation, coaching/reminder, warning, mute, kick, temporary ban, permanent ban recommendation, rollback/repair or repayment, or referral to another SOP. Explain which rule and evidence support the decision.

### 4.6 Notify parties
- Tell the reported player the rule, action, duration, and appeal route.
- Tell the reporter the matter was reviewed, but do not expose private account, punishment-history, or staff details.
- Do not require the reporter to confront the accused.

### 4.7 Close and monitor
Document the outcome, link any punishment record, and flag retaliation concerns. Reopen if meaningful new evidence appears.

## 5. When to get senior help

Escalate threats of real-world harm, doxxing, sexual exploitation, extortion, credible account compromise, staff involvement, organized cheating, major economy abuse, or cases likely to require a permanent ban.

## 6. What to record

- Case identifier
- Intake details and evidence
- Investigator and conflict check
- Timeline and findings
- Decision, rule, action, and duration
- Notifications and appeal instructions

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] One case record created
- [ ] Immediate safety considered
- [ ] Investigator is neutral
- [ ] Evidence verified and preserved
- [ ] Reported player context considered where safe
- [ ] Decision tied to a rule
- [ ] Parties notified appropriately
- [ ] Retaliation monitoring noted
