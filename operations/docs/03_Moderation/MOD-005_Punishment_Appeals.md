# MOD-005 — Reviewing Punishment Appeals

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Review punishment appeals fairly and without protecting the original staff decision from honest review.

## 2. Scope

Appeals of warnings where review is meaningful, mutes, temporary bans, permanent bans, content restrictions, forum/Discord actions, and associated repair or repayment decisions.

## 3. Who does what

- **Support staff:** Confirms the appeal is complete and locates the case.
- **Appeal reviewer:** Reviews the record independently.
- **Original moderator:** May clarify facts but does not make the final appeal decision alone.
- **Administrator/Staff Manager:** Approves permanent-ban and complex appeal outcomes.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Intake requirements
Request:
- Player identifiers
- Punishment reason and approximate date
- What outcome they seek
- Why they believe the decision was wrong or should be reduced
- New evidence or relevant context

Reject only clearly abusive duplicates, impersonation, or appeals that contain no identifiable case after reasonable assistance.

### 4.2 Independence
Assign a reviewer who was not the sole issuer and is not personally involved. For staff misconduct or senior staff actions, route to the Staff Manager or Founder.

### 4.3 Review
1. Read the original case and evidence before relying on the appeal narrative.
2. Verify the rule and policy version in effect at the time.
3. Check identity, duration, proportionality, relevant history, and whether procedures were followed.
4. Review new evidence and, when needed, ask one focused follow-up question.
5. Do not require an admission as the only path to reconsideration.

### 4.4 Outcomes
- **Uphold:** Action was supported, fair, and matched the harm.
- **Reduce/modify:** Violation occurred but duration/scope should change.
- **Overturn:** Evidence, identity, rule application, or procedure was materially wrong.
- **Remand:** Further investigation is required before a final outcome.

### 4.5 Response
Write a concise decision that states the outcome, core reason, effective change, and whether further appeal is available. Do not disclose reporters, private logs, anticheat methods, IP data, payment details, or confidential staff discussion.

### 4.6 Repeat appeals
A final appeal may be reconsidered only for meaningful new evidence, a policy change applied retroactively, proof of identity error, or procedural misconduct. Repeated identical submissions may be closed.

## 5. When to get senior help

Escalate permanent bans, credible claims of fabricated evidence, staff conflicts, threats, payment/security overlap, or cases where overturning the action requires significant world/economy restoration.

## 6. What to record

- Appeal submission
- Original case link
- Assigned independent reviewer
- Review notes
- Outcome and approval
- Player response and effective changes

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Case and identity located
- [ ] Independent reviewer assigned
- [ ] Original evidence reviewed
- [ ] New evidence considered
- [ ] Policy at time of action checked
- [ ] Outcome documented
- [ ] Player notified without exposing protected information
- [ ] Punishment systems updated
