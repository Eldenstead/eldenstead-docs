# COM-001 — Announcing Downtime and Server Problems

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Give players clear and honest updates during maintenance, outages, and other server problems.

## 2. Scope

Discord announcements, website notices, in-game messages, status posts, forum notices, and social posts concerning service availability or incidents.

## 3. Who does what

- **Incident/Maintenance Lead:** Supplies confirmed facts and approves technical accuracy.
- **Communications owner:** Writes and publishes updates.
- **Founder:** Approves security/privacy/payment disclosures and major compensation statements.
- **Other staff:** Direct players to the official update rather than speculate.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Initial confirmation
State:
- What service or feature is affected
- When the issue began or maintenance starts
- What players should avoid doing, if anything
- That investigation/work is active
- Where the next update will appear

Avoid blame, root-cause claims, exact restoration promises, and unnecessary technical detail before facts are verified.

### 4.2 Update cadence
Update when there is a meaningful change: scope discovered, workaround available, maintenance extended, service reopening, or recovery complete. During long high-impact incidents, provide periodic confirmation that work continues even if root cause is not yet known.

### 4.3 Wording rules
- Separate confirmed facts from investigation.
- Use absolute dates/times and include timezone for scheduled events.
- Do not reveal exploit steps, login details, private player data, attack indicators that weaken response, or unannounced content.
- Do not promise compensation until approved.
- Correct earlier information visibly rather than silently editing away a major mistake.

### 4.4 Resolution notice
Include restored services, any remaining limitations, actions players should take, status of affected data where confirmed, and support route. A brief apology is appropriate when Eldenstead caused disruption; avoid excessive self-blame before review.

### 4.5 Channel consistency
The Discord announcement or designated status location is the primary source. Mirror concise versions elsewhere and link/point back consistently. Remove stale banners/notices after resolution.

### 4.6 Post-incident explanation
For major incidents, publish a summary only after internal review. Explain impact, broad cause, correction, and prevention without exposing security details or blaming individuals.

## 5. When to get senior help

Founder approval is required before announcing a breach, payment issue, legal matter, staff misconduct outcome, data-loss estimate, wipe/rollback, or compensation plan.

## 6. What to record

- Draft and approver
- Published channels/timestamps
- Corrections
- Final resolution notice
- Links to incident/maintenance record

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Impact and affected service stated
- [ ] Date/time/timezone clear
- [ ] Player action stated if needed
- [ ] No speculation or unsafe detail
- [ ] Update source identified
- [ ] Meaningful changes communicated
- [ ] Resolution and remaining limits published
- [ ] Stale notices removed
