# EVT-002 — Running Admin-Controlled Wars

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> War staff must never use private or administrative information to assist either side.

## 1. Purpose

Run Eldenstead's staff-controlled wars fairly and in the same way each time. Prevent technical abuse and keep both sides informed about goals and rulings.

## 2. Scope

Approved kingdom-versus-kingdom or empire-versus-empire wars using Capture-the-Hill claim objectives, grace periods, capital assaults, boss bars, action bars, gates, and related custom systems.

## 3. Who does what

- **War Administrator:** Owns setup, final checks, rulings, pause/resume, and conclusion.
- **Technical operator:** Configures the war and monitors system state.
- **Moderators:** Enforce general rules and investigate misconduct.
- **Observers/referees:** Record objective state and disputes without assisting a side.
- **Founder:** Approves unusual rules, major consequences, and cancellations.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Approval and war brief
Record participants, eligible kingdoms/empires, start/end conditions, selected objectives, grace periods, capital threshold, allowed gear/mechanics, offline/absence rules, surrender terms, consequences, and appeal authority.

### 4.2 Conflict controls
Staff who belong to, lead, or closely support a kingdom or empire in the war must report that connection. They should not be the only War Administrator or the only person reviewing a dispute.

### 4.3 Technical checks before the war
1. Verify participant membership and leadership.
2. Snapshot relevant kingdom/empire balances, claims, capitals, permissions, and war configuration.
3. Test objective triggers, control percentages, action bars, boss bars, attacker/defender colors, gate interactions, deaths/respawns, logout/rejoin, and restart persistence.
4. Confirm selected KOTH claims are reachable and not inside broken/protected geometry.
5. Back up relevant data and prepare manual pause/rollback commands.

### 4.4 Announcement
Publish exact start time, participants, objective rules, prohibited behavior, staff authority, disconnect/server-failure handling, and where official updates appear. Do not expose hidden tactical data beyond the approved rules.

### 4.5 Running the war
- Start only after both technical and rules preflight pass.
- Maintain an objective timeline and record automatic/manual interventions.
- Pause for server failure, objective malfunction, major exploit, or widespread inability to participate.
- Do not pause for normal tactical disadvantage.
- Staff must not reveal enemy positions, private chat, hidden routes, or admin-only state.

### 4.6 Disputes
Capture logs/video/objective state. The War Administrator may make immediate rulings to continue play; consequential rulings should receive independent review. Correct system errors consistently for both sides.

### 4.7 Ending the war
Verify the winning condition from system records and observer evidence. Apply only approved consequences. Announce the result, restore temporary settings, archive the war record, and review technical/rules issues before another war.

## 5. When to get senior help

Escalate exploit use, staff bias, corrupted claims/balances, contradictory system state, major outage, unsafe real-world threats, or any consequence not approved before the war.

## 6. What to record

- War brief and participant snapshot
- Conflict disclosures
- Technical preflight
- Objective/event timeline
- Pause/ruling evidence
- Final state and applied consequences
- Post-war review

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Participants and rules approved
- [ ] Staff conflicts disclosed
- [ ] Claims/capitals/balances snapshotted
- [ ] Objective UI and restart behavior tested
- [ ] Backup/pause/rollback ready
- [ ] Public rules announced
- [ ] Interventions recorded neutrally
- [ ] Winning condition verified
- [ ] Temporary state cleaned up
