# EVT-001 — Planning Community Events

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Plan and run community events that are fair, safe, and enjoyable.

## 2. Scope

Contests, festivals, hunts, races, roleplay gatherings, building events, server-wide activities, giveaways, and limited-time content.

## 3. Who does what

- **Event Lead:** Owns scope, runbook, staff assignments, and final event decisions.
- **Lore/Build owners:** Approve setting, locations, and canon.
- **Administrator/Developer:** Reviews commands, plugins, rewards, and performance.
- **Moderator:** Handles conduct and disputes.
- **Communications owner:** Publishes rules, time, and results.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Event brief
Define purpose, audience, date/time/timezone, estimated duration, location, capacity, entry method, rules, scoring, rewards, accessibility, staff roles, technical dependencies, and cancellation criteria.

### 4.2 Fairness review
- Publish objective rules before participation begins.
- Identify whether teams, kingdoms, empires, gear, skills, time zones, or network latency create advantages.
- Do not allow staff participants to access hidden scoring or admin tools.
- Separate judges from close friends/teams when practical.
- Ensure store purchases do not create event-winning advantages.

### 4.3 Technical preparation
1. Build/test the location outside live risk when practical.
2. Test commands, checkpoints, teleports, inventories, deaths, disconnects, rewards, and cleanup.
3. Estimate entity/player/chunk load.
4. Create a backup or rollback point for world-changing events.
5. Prepare fallback/manual scoring and a cancellation message.

### 4.4 Runbook
Use `Event_Runbook_Template.md`. Include opening script, staff positions, timing, commands, score recording, rule enforcement, contingency actions, reward delivery, and cleanup.

### 4.5 Running the war
One Event Lead controls the timeline. Moderation remains separate from judging where possible. Pause when a technical defect materially affects fairness. Record disputed results and avoid deciding complex appeals in public chat.

### 4.6 Rewards
Define exact rewards and delivery method in advance. Record recipients and commands/transactions. Avoid unique gameplay advantages or economy injections large enough to distort normal play without Founder approval.

### 4.7 Closeout
Announce results, deliver verified rewards, remove temporary permissions/entities/builds, restore inventories/settings, archive scores, and review feedback/incidents.

## 5. When to get senior help

Escalate real-money or store-linked prizes, major economy rewards, event-wide technical failure, accusations of staff favoritism, unsafe player behavior, or lore/war consequences beyond the approved brief.

## 6. What to record

- Event brief and approvals
- Participant rules
- Runbook and staff roster
- Backup/rollback point
- Scores/results
- Reward delivery record
- Incident/feedback review

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Purpose, rules, time, and rewards approved
- [ ] Fairness/conflict review complete
- [ ] Technical test and load review passed
- [ ] Runbook and fallback ready
- [ ] Staff roles assigned
- [ ] Results and disputes recorded
- [ ] Rewards delivered consistently
- [ ] Temporary content/access cleaned up
