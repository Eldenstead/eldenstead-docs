# PRV-001 — Player Privacy and Data Requests

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Do not reveal IP addresses, private reports, or another player’s information to prove a moderation decision.

## 1. Purpose

Handle player privacy and data requests safely and send legal questions to the Founders.

## 2. Scope

Minecraft/Discord identifiers, email addresses, IP/log data, website/forum accounts, tickets, moderation history, store references, analytics, media, and requests to access, correct, or delete personal information.

## 3. Who does what

- **Support:** Receives requests and verifies basic scope without exposing data.
- **Privacy owner/Founder:** Determines applicable response and approves disclosure/deletion.
- **System owners:** Search, export, correct, restrict, or delete data as instructed.
- **All staff:** Limit access and avoid unnecessary copying.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Data minimization
Collect only information needed for server operation, safety, moderation, support, payments, and legal obligations. Do not build informal dossiers or copy private data into general staff channels.

### 4.2 Request intake
Ask the requester to identify the account(s), type of request, and systems involved. Do not ask for passwords or excessive identity documents. Use reasonable account control/history checks and escalate uncertain identity.

### 4.3 Request categories
- **Access:** Summary or copy of relevant data held about the requester.
- **Correction:** Fix inaccurate account information.
- **Deletion:** Remove data that is not required to retain.
- **Restriction/objection:** Review certain processing or communication.
- **Consent/media:** Review use of voice, testimonial, or focused promotional media.

### 4.4 Review limitations
Before disclosure or deletion, protect:
- Other players' personal information
- Reporter/witness confidentiality
- Security and anticheat methods
- Legal/payment/fraud records that must be retained
- Active moderation/appeal/security investigations
- Backups that cannot be selectively edited immediately, subject to normal expiration and non-restoration except disaster recovery

Do not promise complete deletion before the Privacy Owner reviews storage needs.

### 4.5 Fulfillment
Search only relevant systems, record sources, redact third-party data, use a secure delivery method, and document actions. Correct authoritative records rather than creating conflicting copies.

### 4.6 Staff access and sharing
IP addresses, emails, payment references, private tickets, and security logs are need-to-know. Do not expose them in punishment reasons, public forums, screenshots, or normal moderation chat.

### 4.7 Incidents
Accidental disclosure, unauthorized access, or lost exports follow SEC-003 immediately. Preserve facts and do not privately negotiate secrecy with the affected person.

## 5. When to get senior help

All formal access/deletion requests, minors' data concerns, legal demands, payment/fraud storage, data breaches, or uncertainty about applicable law require Founder/Privacy Owner review.

## 6. What to record

- Request and account identifiers
- Identity verification method
- Systems searched
- Data redacted/retained and reason
- Fulfillment/deletion/correction actions
- Delivery and closure date

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Request scope and identity reviewed
- [ ] No excessive login details/documents requested
- [ ] Relevant systems identified
- [ ] Third-party/security data protected
- [ ] Storage limitations reviewed
- [ ] Secure fulfillment used
- [ ] Actions documented
- [ ] Any disclosure incident escalated
