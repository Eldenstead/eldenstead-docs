# LOR-001 — Approving Official Lore

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Decide what lore is official and keep new lore consistent with the Eldenstead world.

## 2. Scope

Official lore pages, NPC dialogue, books, quests, locations, gods, factions, kingdoms/empires, events, item descriptions, forum canon, and marketing statements presented as factual world lore.

## 3. Who does what

- **Lore writer:** Drafts content and marks assumptions/open questions.
- **Lore lead:** Maintains canon index and reviews consistency.
- **Gameplay/build owner:** Confirms content can be implemented and experienced as intended.
- **Founder:** Approves major cosmology, world-history, permanent faction, or central-story changes.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Classify content
- **Official:** Official and binding until formally revised.
- **Soft canon:** Approved interpretation that may be refined.
- **Rumor/in-world belief:** A character or culture believes it; not guaranteed true.
- **Player-created lore:** Belongs to player storytelling and is not official world canon unless adopted.
- **Draft/unannounced:** Internal only.

### 4.2 Lore proposal
Include title, content type, affected canon, intended audience, where it appears, gameplay/build dependencies, spoiler level, and unresolved questions.

### 4.3 Consistency review
Check names, dates, geography, culture, technology, magic, deity behavior, previous public statements, and existing item/NPC/location text. For divine/Whispering Heart content, preserve established boundaries and avoid giving ordinary NPCs impossible knowledge without explanation.

### 4.4 Approval levels
- Minor local dialogue/flavor consistent with canon: Lore Lead.
- Named location, recurring NPC, cultural practice, item backstory: Lore Lead plus relevant owner.
- Major deity, world history, cosmology, central faction, or retcon: Founder approval.
- Public release date or gameplay feature embedded in lore: Content/Founder approval.

### 4.5 Implementation packet
Approved lore should include exact text, formatting/color codes if relevant, delivery location, NPC/item/book identifiers, pronunciation notes for important names, and whether players may quote/post it publicly.

### 4.6 Retcons
Prefer additive clarification over erasing public history. When a retcon is necessary, record old canon, new canon, reason, affected content, migration steps, and whether a public explanation is needed.

### 4.7 Player lore
Encourage player kingdoms/empires and characters while separating their claims from official server canon. Staff moderation should address rule violations, not whether a player's fictional interpretation is “correct,” unless it falsely presents itself as official canon.

## 5. When to get senior help

Escalate contradictions involving central lore, proposed real-world hate/harassment themes, sexual content involving minors, use of a player's work without permission, or lore that reveals unannounced systems/dates.

## 6. What to record

- Lore proposal and classification
- Canon references checked
- Reviewer/approver
- Final implementation text
- Dependencies and spoiler level
- Retcon record where applicable

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Content classification assigned
- [ ] Existing canon checked
- [ ] Gameplay/build dependencies confirmed
- [ ] Correct approval level obtained
- [ ] Exact final text stored
- [ ] Spoiler/public status marked
- [ ] Canon index updated
- [ ] Retcons propagated to affected content
