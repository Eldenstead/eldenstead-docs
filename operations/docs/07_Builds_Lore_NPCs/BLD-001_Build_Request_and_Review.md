# BLD-001 — Requesting and Reviewing Builds

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Plan, build, review, and approve world changes before they are opened to players.

## 2. Scope

Spawn, towns, kingdoms/empires areas, dungeons, roads, caves, event arenas, interiors, ships, NPC locations, and other staff-created world content.

## 3. Who does what

- **Requester:** Defines purpose and gameplay needs.
- **Build lead:** Assigns builder, scope, palette, and review stages.
- **Builder:** Works within boundaries and records dependencies.
- **Lore/gameplay reviewer:** Confirms canon and gameplay function.
- **Administrator:** Approves production paste/large edit and technical safety.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Build brief
Before work begins, define:
- Name and purpose
- World/region and boundaries
- Intended player flow and capacity
- Required NPCs, shops, gates, claims, war/event functions, or redstone
- Style references, palette, age/condition, and lore context
- Spoiler classification
- Performance constraints
- Completion criteria and review owner

### 4.2 Work environment
Use a build world, schematic, or isolated region for substantial work. Mark boundaries. Avoid modifying live player areas or official locations before approval.

### 4.3 Review stages
1. **Blockout:** Scale, silhouette, routes, entrances, and gameplay spaces.
2. **Structural review:** Palette, shape, terrain integration, accessibility, and sightlines.
3. **Detail review:** Interiors, lighting, decoration, signage, NPC positions, and atmosphere.
4. **Technical review:** Entity count, redstone, block entities, collision, spawn safety, WorldGuard/claims, and performance.
5. **Final canon/gameplay review:** Names, lore, secrets, routes, shops, event triggers, and player permissions.

### 4.4 Quality checks
- Players can enter/exit without traps unless intentional and approved.
- No unintended voids, exposed commands/signs, barriers, debug blocks, or staff chests.
- Lighting/spawning behavior is intentional.
- Important routes are readable without excessive signage.
- Builds do not reveal unannounced lore/features from public viewpoints.
- NPC interaction points are accessible and protected from obstruction.
- Large decorative entity/model use is justified.

### 4.5 Production move
Follow BLD-002 for large edits. Take a backup/schematic of the destination. Paste with an appropriate air/replace strategy, verify terrain and nearby player content, then complete a walk-through in survival/adventure-like conditions.

### 4.6 Acceptance and handoff
Record final location, schematic/source, dependencies, NPC IDs, protections, maintenance notes, and screenshots. The build is not complete until associated configuration/NPC/lore tasks are assigned.

## 5. When to get senior help

Escalate overlap with player claims/content, major terrain replacement, canon conflict, performance risk, use of unlicensed assets, or a production paste without a verified rollback.

## 6. What to record

- Build brief and boundaries
- Review screenshots/notes
- Schematic/source location
- Destination backup
- Approval and production location
- Dependency/NPC/protection handoff

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Brief and boundaries approved
- [ ] Build created outside live risk area
- [ ] Blockout and style reviewed
- [ ] Technical/performance review passed
- [ ] Canon/gameplay review passed
- [ ] Destination backup/schematic ready
- [ ] Production walk-through completed
- [ ] Dependencies and maintenance documented
