# CNT-001 — Protecting Unannounced Content

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Anything sent to a Minecraft client through a resource pack or plugin message should be treated as discoverable.

## 1. Purpose

Keep unreleased features, builds, lore, dates, and staff discussions private.

## 2. Scope

Draft code, staging servers, screenshots, recordings, documents, private channels, builds visible from public areas, resource-pack assets, NPCs, release plans, and staff conversations.

## 3. Who does what

- **Content owner:** Assigns spoiler level and release plan.
- **All staff/testers:** Share only on a need-to-know basis.
- **Builders/developers:** Prevent accidental public exposure through worlds, logs, assets, commands, or repositories.
- **Founder:** Approves embargo lifting and leak response.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Classification
- **Internal:** Routine staff information not intended for players.
- **Embargoed:** Planned for public release at a defined time/event.
- **Restricted:** High-impact feature, security-sensitive detail, partnership, or major lore that only assigned staff need.
- **Public:** Officially released through an approved channel.

### 4.2 Handling rules
- Use private channels and access-controlled repositories/storage.
- Do not post screenshots containing hidden builds, coordinates, code, console, tabs, file paths, or future assets.
- Keep staging whitelisted and separate from public server lists where practical.
- Avoid including secret content in resource packs or client files before intended release; assume players can inspect anything downloaded.
- Use codenames only when helpful; do not rely on obscurity as security.

### 4.3 Testing with players
Select testers deliberately, explain confidentiality, limit access to the necessary feature, and remove access afterward. Do not promise rewards or permanent status unless approved.

### 4.4 Public teasers
A teaser should have an approved asset, caption, release time, and list of details that must remain hidden. Avoid accidental promises, exact dates not yet committed, or showing features that may be removed.

### 4.5 Accidental leak
1. Preserve the post/message and identify what was exposed.
2. Remove or restrict it where practical without creating more attention.
3. Notify the content owner/Founder.
4. Decide whether to continue embargo, clarify publicly, or release early.
5. Review how access/process allowed the leak.

Do not publicly shame the person or invent denial when the material is authentic.

### 4.6 Deliberate leak
Treat as potential staff misconduct/security issue. Preserve evidence, contain access, and use MOD-007/SEC-003. Public communication remains factual and limited.

## 5. When to get senior help

Escalate leaks involving login details, player personal data, security weaknesses, payment/provider details, legal agreements, or content that could enable exploitation.

## 6. What to record

- Content classification and owner
- Approved tester/access list
- Embargo/release date
- Approved teaser assets
- Leak evidence, containment, and decision
- Post-leak corrective actions

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Spoiler level assigned
- [ ] Access limited to need-to-know
- [ ] Public-visible worlds/assets checked
- [ ] Tester access documented and removed
- [ ] Teasers approved before posting
- [ ] Leaks preserved and contained
- [ ] Security/privacy overlap escalated
