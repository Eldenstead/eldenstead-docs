# SUP-001 — Handling Support Tickets

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Give players clear and respectful help while keeping ticket records organized.

## 2. Scope

Discord support tickets, website contact requests, in-game help escalations, billing inquiries, bug reports, appeals routed to support, and internal referrals.

## 3. Who does what

- **Helper/Support:** Owns intake, routine answers, categorization, and follow-up.
- **Moderator:** Handles rule, report, and punishment issues.
- **Developer/Administrator:** Handles confirmed technical or live-service issues.
- **Billing owner/Founder:** Handles payment disputes, refunds, and sensitive purchase information.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Intake and categorization
Assign one primary category:
- General gameplay or server information
- Technical support
- Bug report
- Player report
- Punishment appeal
- Store purchase or missing item/perk
- Account/access problem
- Staff/leadership concern

Rename or tag the ticket consistently and identify the assigned owner.

### 4.2 First response
1. Acknowledge the request and restate the issue briefly.
2. Ask only for information needed to proceed.
3. Never ask for passwords, login codes, full payment-card details, or recovery codes.
4. Set expectations without inventing an exact resolution time.
5. If the issue belongs elsewhere, transfer it with a summary rather than making the player repeat everything.

### 4.3 Investigation and updates
- Use official records and current SOPs.
- Keep internal notes separate from shown to players messages where the system permits.
- Give an update when ownership changes, additional information is needed, or the issue becomes a known incident.
- Avoid repeated “we are looking into it” messages with no useful change.

### 4.4 Resolution
A resolution message should state:
- What was found or what guidance applies
- What was changed, restored, denied, or escalated
- Any player action still required
- Related rule, guide, or appeal route

Do not expose private reports, internal logs, staff discussion, exploit details, IP data, or another player's punishment.

### 4.5 Closure
Close when the request is resolved, transferred into a tracked incident/task, withdrawn, or inactive after reasonable follow-up. Before closing:
1. Summarize the outcome.
2. Ensure required actions were completed.
3. Add internal tags and links.
4. Invite the player to open a new ticket if a materially different issue appears.

### 4.6 Reopened and duplicate tickets
Merge or reference duplicates. Do not punish a player for opening a duplicate unless they are deliberately spamming or evading restrictions. Keep the most complete ticket as the primary record.

## 5. When to get senior help

Get senior help at once for threats, doxxing, staff misconduct, security concerns, payment chargebacks, legal demands, widespread outages, or any request for production login details or personal data.

## 6. What to record

- Ticket category and owner
- Player identifiers
- Requested information and evidence
- Internal actions and transfers
- Resolution summary
- Closure reason and date

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Ticket categorized
- [ ] Owner assigned
- [ ] No prohibited sensitive information requested
- [ ] Relevant SOP followed
- [ ] Player received meaningful updates
- [ ] Resolution summarized
- [ ] Internal records linked
- [ ] Ticket closed or transferred cleanly
