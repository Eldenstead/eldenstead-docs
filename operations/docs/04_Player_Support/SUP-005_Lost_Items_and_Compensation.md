# SUP-005 — Lost Items and Fair Compensation

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Replace lost items only when the loss can be checked and compensation is fair.

## 2. Scope

Claims of lost items, currency, purchases, experience, claims, kingdom/empire funds, custom items, or progress caused by confirmed server faults, rollback, exploit cleanup, or staff error.

## 3. Who does what

- **Support:** Collects the claim and timestamps.
- **Moderator:** Separates normal gameplay loss, theft, and rule violations.
- **Administrator:** Verifies logs/backups and authorizes routine restoration.
- **Founder:** Approves broad or high-value compensation, policy exceptions, and uncertain mass-loss events.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Eligibility
Compensation may be considered when loss was caused by:
- Confirmed server crash, rollback, plugin defect, or data corruption
- Verified staff mistake
- Approved exploit cleanup affecting legitimate assets
- Confirmed store delivery failure
- An announced event error where staff explicitly accepted responsibility

Normally ineligible:
- Normal death, despawn, combat, war, economy risk, or player mistake
- Unverified claims with no supporting record
- Loss caused by prohibited mods, exploits, account sharing, or ban evasion
- Market price changes or voluntary trades

### 4.2 Collect the claim
Record item/currency type, amount, custom identifiers/enchants where relevant, approximate time, location/container/account, event leading to loss, and evidence.

### 4.3 Verify
Use inventory logs, economy logs, container history, command logs, purchase records, backups, event records, and crash timeline. Verify both ownership and disappearance. Check whether the item/value still exists elsewhere to avoid duplication.

### 4.4 Decide restoration method
Prefer, in order:
1. Restore the exact item/value from reliable records.
2. Restore a conservative equivalent when exact metadata is known well enough.
3. Offer standardized incident compensation approved for all similarly affected players.
4. Deny when the claim cannot be reasonably verified.

Do not substitute rare, lore-significant, unobtainable, or staff-only items without explicit approval.

### 4.5 Deliver safely
Use an approved command/mailbox/process while the player is identified. Record exact commands or transaction IDs. For high-value delivery, use a second staff reviewer or screenshot/log verification.

### 4.6 Mass incidents
Create one incident compensation plan with eligibility, calculation, delivery method, deadline, and public wording. Avoid negotiating different rewards privately with individual players.

## 5. When to get senior help

Escalate major kingdom/empire treasury restoration, widespread economy loss, lore-unique items, uncertain duplication risk, high-value store purchases, or requests connected to staff misconduct.

## 6. What to record

- Claim and player identifiers
- Loss details and incident link
- Verification sources
- Approval and calculation
- Delivery commands/records
- Duplicate check and final response

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Loss type is eligible
- [ ] Ownership and disappearance checked
- [ ] Duplicate value ruled out
- [ ] Exact or conservative restoration chosen
- [ ] Required approval obtained
- [ ] Delivery recorded
- [ ] Similar players treated consistently
- [ ] Ticket closed with explanation
