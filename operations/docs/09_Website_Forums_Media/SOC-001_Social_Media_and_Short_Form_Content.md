# SOC-001 — Social Media and Short Videos

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Create clear social posts and short videos without leaking private or unreleased content.

## 2. Scope

YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, Discord promos, Minecraft forum ads, partnership posts, shorts, trailers, clips, captions, and community recruitment posts.

## 3. Who does what

- **Content lead:** Owns calendar, briefs, approvals, and account access.
- **Creator/editor:** Captures and edits approved material.
- **Reviewer:** Checks accuracy, spoilers, branding, audio/media rights, and links.
- **Founder:** Approves major campaigns, paid partnerships, release claims, and sensitive responses.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Content brief
Define platform, audience, one main message, footage/assets needed, call to action, publication date, spoiler boundaries, and success metric. Short-form content should usually show one clear feature or feeling rather than explain the entire server.

### 4.2 Capture
Use approved builds, accounts, skins, names, and UI. Hide staff tools, private chat, coordinates, debug screens, unreleased items, and personal notifications. Record in a test/cinematic environment when practical.

### 4.3 Accuracy
- Do not stage features that players cannot actually access unless clearly labeled concept/WIP.
- Avoid claims such as “best,” guaranteed release dates, player counts, or unique features without support.
- Use the correct Discord and website links.
- Describe the custom map accurately; do not imply every location is custom-built if that is not true.
- Keep non-pay-to-win messaging consistent with actual store products.

### 4.4 Rights and consent
Use music, fonts, art, mods, maps, skins, and clips only when licensing allows the intended platform/commercial use. Obtain permission before featuring a player as the clear subject of a testimonial, voice, or private interaction. Follow MED-001.

### 4.5 Review and publish
Check opening hook, legibility, captions, audio level, crop/safe area, factual claims, spoiler frames, usernames/chat, links, and platform rules. Save the project/source and final export. Publish through official accounts; do not share master login details with contractors.

### 4.6 Community responses
Reply politely and briefly. Do not argue with critics, reveal internal plans, or make feature commitments in comments. Route support/moderation issues to official channels.

### 4.7 Metrics and iteration
Record views, storage, clicks/joins where available, and qualitative feedback. Reuse successful formats without reposting identical spam across communities. Respect each forum/Discord's advertising rules.

## 5. When to get senior help

Escalate paid promotions/partnerships, copyright claims, platform strikes, leaked content, impersonation accounts, harassment campaigns, or any creator requesting direct access to production/private staff material.

## 6. What to record

- Content brief/calendar
- Source assets and rights
- Consent where required
- Review approval
- Published links/date
- Metrics and lessons
- Contractor access record

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] One message and audience defined
- [ ] Footage has no private/spoiler material
- [ ] Claims and links verified
- [ ] Media rights confirmed
- [ ] Consent obtained where needed
- [ ] Final platform crop/captions checked
- [ ] Account access remains individual/controlled
- [ ] Results recorded
