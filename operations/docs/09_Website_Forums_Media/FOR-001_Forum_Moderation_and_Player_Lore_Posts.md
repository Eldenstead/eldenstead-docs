# FOR-001 — Moderating Forums and Player Lore

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Keep forums organized and safe while supporting player-created stories and screenshots.

## 2. Scope

Website forums and related Discord-linked discussion areas, including player lore, kingdom/empire posts, screenshots, guides, suggestions, recruitment, and support-adjacent posts.

## 3. Who does what

- **Forum moderators:** Enforce forum rules, organize threads, and preserve evidence.
- **Lore team:** Clarifies canon status without overriding player creativity unnecessarily.
- **Support/Moderation:** Moves reports, appeals, billing, and private issues into proper tickets.
- **Community lead:** Approves categories, pinned posts, and major moderation decisions.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Category and thread placement
Keep categories simple and clear. Move misplaced threads with a brief redirect when useful. Do not delete good-faith content merely because it belongs elsewhere.

### 4.2 Player lore status
Player-created lore is non-official unless officially adopted. Players may describe their characters, kingdoms, empires, histories, beliefs, and events, provided they do not:
- Claim control over official server canon or staff decisions
- Write actions for another player without consent
- Reveal private/unannounced staff material
- Use lore to evade harassment, hate, sexual-content, impersonation, or other rules
- Misrepresent a player-run organization as official Eldenstead staff

Use labels such as **Player Lore**, **Official Lore**, **Rumor**, or **Archive** where available.

### 4.3 Moderation process
1. Preserve the relevant post/thread and context.
2. Apply the least disruptive correction: edit request, move, tag, lock, hide, or remove.
3. Explain the rule and how to repost where possible.
4. Use MOD-004 for punishments rather than improvising forum-only consequences.

### 4.4 Screenshots and media
Remove content exposing private staff areas, personal information, exploit instructions, hidden coordinates/content, or non-consensual harassment. Normal public gameplay screenshots do not require individual permission, but targeted use should follow MED-001.

### 4.5 Necroposting and bumps
Allow reasonable updates and periodic bumps where categories permit. Prevent spam by defining a minimum bump interval rather than subjective enforcement.

### 4.6 Locked/archived threads
Lock when discussion has become unproductive, the issue moved to a ticket, the event ended, or the post must remain as a record. Archive valuable guides and completed lore rather than deleting them.

### 4.7 Official adoption of player content
Obtain the creator's permission, clarify credit/rights, perform LOR-001 review, and record whether Eldenstead may edit or reuse the content.

## 5. When to get senior help

Escalate doxxing, threats, sexual exploitation, copyright disputes, staff impersonation, leaked restricted content, or a request to adopt/use player work commercially.

## 6. What to record

- Post/thread link
- Preserved evidence
- Moderation action/reason
- Canon label or adoption permission
- User notification and appeal route

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Context preserved
- [ ] Correct category/label used
- [ ] Player lore kept distinct from official canon
- [ ] Least disruptive action selected
- [ ] Private/report issues moved to tickets
- [ ] Punishment follows MOD-004
- [ ] Creator permission recorded for official adoption
