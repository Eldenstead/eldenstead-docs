# MED-001 — Screenshots, Recordings, and Permission

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Use screenshots, recordings, voices, and player-made media with care and permission.

## 2. Scope

Screenshots, video, livestreams, voice recordings, interviews, testimonials, cinematic footage, staff calls, support interactions, and community submissions.

## 3. Who does what

- **Recorder/creator:** Reviews the scene and obtains required consent.
- **Content lead:** Approves official use and storage.
- **Moderator/support lead:** Protects case/ticket evidence from promotional use.
- **Participants:** May set reasonable boundaries for voice/interview/testimonial use.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Normal public gameplay
Incidental capture of players in normal public gameplay is generally acceptable for server promotion, but creators should avoid embarrassing, targeted, or misleading presentation. Hide private messages, account notifications, coordinates of sensitive locations, and personal desktop information.

### 4.2 Consent required
Obtain clear permission before using:
- A player's voice, interview, or testimonial
- A player as the primary identifiable subject of a scripted promotional scene
- Private Discord calls, tickets, DMs, staff meetings, or support interactions
- A creator's submitted art/video beyond the permission they granted
- Content involving minors in a focused promotional or interview context

### 4.3 Capture checklist
- Use an approved server/build and time.
- Disable or hide private chat and staff overlays.
- Check tab list, usernames, coordinates, minimap, browser tabs, notifications, and file paths.
- Avoid unreleased locations/assets unless the brief allows them.
- Use spectator/cinematic tools only with approval and without affecting players.

### 4.4 Editing
Do not fabricate player statements, rearrange events to falsely accuse someone, or present staging footage as live gameplay without context. Blur/redact private information and remove unapproved voice/chat.

### 4.5 Storage
Store raw footage in approved team storage with access limited to creators/editors. Raw files may contain more private information than the final edit. Set deletion/storage expectations for interviews and unused sensitive footage.

### 4.6 Community submissions
Record the submitter, permission granted, credit preference, allowed edits, platforms, and whether commercial promotion is permitted. Do not assume posting in Discord transfers ownership.

### 4.7 Revocation concerns
When someone reasonably asks to remove focused voice/interview/testimonial content, route to the Content Lead. Removal may not always be possible from third-party reposts, but Eldenstead should act on content it controls where appropriate.

## 5. When to get senior help

Escalate accidental publication of personal data, private tickets/calls, sexual content involving minors, doxxing, copyright disputes, or raw footage showing login details/security systems.

## 6. What to record

- Media brief
- Participant consent/release record
- Source/creator rights
- Raw/final storage location
- Redaction/review notes
- Publication and removal requests

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Scene and capture tool approved
- [ ] Private overlays/messages hidden
- [ ] Focused voice/interview consent obtained
- [ ] Spoilers and coordinates checked
- [ ] Edit remains truthful
- [ ] Raw footage access restricted
- [ ] Submission rights/credit recorded
- [ ] Final review completed
