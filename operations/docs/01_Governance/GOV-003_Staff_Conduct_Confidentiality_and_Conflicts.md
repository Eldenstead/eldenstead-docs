# GOV-003 — Staff Conduct, Privacy, and Conflicts

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Staff access is a responsibility, not a gameplay advantage or social status.

## 1. Purpose

Set clear rules for staff behavior, private information, fairness, and personal conflicts.

## 2. Scope

Staff activity in-game, on Discord, on the website, in private staff spaces, in direct messages related to Eldenstead, and on external platforms when speaking as an Eldenstead representative.

## 3. Who does what

- **All staff:** Follow this SOP and report serious violations.
- **Team leads:** Correct minor issues and document repeated concerns.
- **Staff Manager:** Investigates conduct and conflicts.
- **Founders:** Review serious allegations, removal decisions, and matters involving the Staff Manager.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Conduct expectations
Staff must be respectful, truthful, and fair. Do not humiliate players, argue publicly while using staff authority, threaten punishments, retaliate for criticism, or use private information for entertainment.

### 4.2 Confidential information
Treat the following as internal unless formally published:
- Unannounced features, dates, builds, lore, plugins, and store plans
- Staff applications, private reports, appeals, and disciplinary records
- IP addresses, account identifiers, payment references, email addresses, and security logs
- Host details, login details, tokens, private code repository contents, backups, and configuration secrets
- Internal disagreements, incident notes, and unreleased media

Share internal information only with people who need it for their assigned work. Do not copy it into personal servers, public AI tools, paste sites, or unsecured cloud storage.

### 4.3 Conflicts of interest
1. Disclose close personal relationships, financial interests, or active disputes that could affect a decision.
2. Do not moderate your own report, your close friend's case, a rival kingdom's dispute, or an appeal of your own punishment action.
3. Transfer the matter to an uninvolved staff member.
4. Do not use staff knowledge to benefit your own kingdom, empire, economy position, event result, or trading activity.

### 4.4 Staff gameplay
- Staff playing normally must follow the same gameplay rules as players.
- Administrative tools may not be used for personal gain.
- Testing items, currency, permissions, teleportation, or information must be isolated from normal gameplay and removed after testing.
- Staff should clearly separate official actions from roleplay.

### 4.5 Gifts and payments
Do not accept money, items, services, ranks, or favors in exchange for moderation, access, leaked information, build approval, or support priority. Small in-game roleplay gifts may be accepted only when they provide no meaningful advantage and do not affect staff judgment.

### 4.6 Reporting concerns
Use the staff misconduct process. Preserve messages and logs. Do not start a public campaign or confront the accused in a way that risks evidence deletion.

## 5. When to get senior help

Serious confidentiality breaches, retaliation, discrimination, payment-related misconduct, login detail sharing, or abuse of production access must be escalated directly to a Founder. Allegations involving a Founder should be documented and reviewed by the other Founder or an independent trusted reviewer where possible.

## 6. What to record

- Conflict disclosure
- Conduct coaching note or formal report
- Evidence links and timestamps
- Investigation outcome and access changes
- Confidentiality confirmation

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Confidential information limited to need-to-know access
- [ ] Conflicts disclosed and reassigned
- [ ] Staff tools not used for personal benefit
- [ ] Gifts or favors declined where judgment could be affected
- [ ] Serious concerns documented and escalated privately
