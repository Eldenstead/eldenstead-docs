# GOV-001 — Staff Roles, Authority, and Getting Help

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Do not use another person’s account, token, console session, or login details to bypass your assigned authority.

## 1. Purpose

Explain who can make decisions, use high-level staff tools, approve unusual cases, and speak for Eldenstead. Clear limits help prevent mixed instructions and misuse of staff power.

## 2. Scope

All staff, contractors, volunteers, and temporary event helpers across the Minecraft server, Discord, website, development repositories, host panels, and social accounts.

## 3. Who does what

- **Founders:** Final authority for server direction, finances, legal matters, permanent production access, and unusual moderation decisions.
- **Staff Manager:** Owns staffing, training, staff conduct, normal requests for senior help, and work between teams.
- **Administrator:** Leads live incidents, senior moderation, approved rollback actions, and operational decisions when a founder is unavailable.
- **Developer:** Investigates and changes code or configuration within approved environments and release procedures.
- **Moderator:** Enforces public rules using the documented discipline matrix.
- **Helper / Support:** Handles questions, collects facts, and routes issues without making promises outside assigned authority.
- **Builder / Lore / Event / Content roles:** Control only their approved work area; they do not automatically gain moderation or production authority.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Confirm authority before acting
1. Identify the task, affected service, and potential impact.
2. Check whether the action is reversible and whether it affects players, money, permissions, data, or announced content.
3. Confirm that your role is authorized in the matrix below.
4. If the action exceeds your authority, gather the information needed and escalate rather than borrowing another staff member's account.

### 4.2 Default approval matrix

| Action | Minimum role | Additional approval |
|---|---|---|
| Answer a normal player question | Helper | None |
| Close a normal support ticket | Helper | None |
| Issue a warning or short mute | Moderator | Follow MOD-004 |
| Temporary ban up to 7 days | Moderator | Evidence required |
| Temporary ban over 7 days | Administrator | Senior review |
| Permanent ban | Administrator | Founder or Staff Manager approval, except immediate security threats |
| Restore items | Administrator / authorized support lead | Follow SUP-005 |
| Restart production | Administrator / authorized developer | Follow OPS-001 |
| Roll back world or database data | Administrator | Founder approval unless active data destruction continues |
| Install/update a production plugin | Developer / Administrator | Approved change request |
| Change staff permissions | Staff Manager / Founder | Follow OPS-007 |
| Publish an unannounced feature | Content lead | Founder approval |
| Promise or issue a refund | Founder / authorized billing owner | Follow SUP-003 and posted terms |
| Change privacy, terms, or rules | Founder | Written review and public effective date |

### 4.3 Escalation path
1. **Routine:** Assigned team lead.
2. **Cross-team or disputed:** Staff Manager or Administrator.
3. **Security, payment, privacy, permanent access, legal, or major reputation risk:** Founder.
4. **Immediate danger:** Contain the issue first using the least destructive action, then notify senior staff.

### 4.4 Incident command
For SEV-1 or SEV-2 events, one person is named **Incident Lead**. Other staff provide updates to that person rather than issuing competing instructions. The Incident Lead records decisions, assigns tasks, and decides when normal operations resume.

### 4.5 Public communication
Only the assigned Incident Lead, Staff Manager, Founder, or designated communications staff member should publish official explanations. Other staff may acknowledge that the issue is known but must not speculate.

## 5. When to get senior help

Get senior help whenever authority is unclear, staff members disagree on an active response, the action may create irreversible loss, or the issue involves staff misconduct. If no senior person is immediately available during an active threat, contain access or disable the affected feature without deleting evidence.

## 6. What to record

- Decision or incident ticket
- Approver name and timestamp
- Relevant evidence and affected accounts
- Actions taken and any rollback point
- Public statement, if one was issued

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Task and impact identified
- [ ] Required role and approval confirmed
- [ ] One decision owner assigned
- [ ] Action documented
- [ ] Player communication approved when needed
- [ ] Exception reviewed after the event
