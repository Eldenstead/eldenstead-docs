# STF-003 — Training and Trial Period

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Train new staff, review their work, and decide whether they are ready for full staff status.

## 2. Scope

All newly accepted staff and existing staff transferring into a substantially different or more high-level role.

## 3. Who does what

- **Probation lead:** Sets goals, reviews work, and provides feedback.
- **New staff member:** Completes training honestly and reports mistakes quickly.
- **Staff Manager:** Approves extension, completion, reassignment, or removal.
- **Founders:** Approve completion into senior high-level roles.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Set the probation plan
Default probation is 30 days, adjusted for role activity. Record:
- Required SOPs and tools
- Three to five observable goals
- Actions the member may perform independently
- Actions requiring supervision
- Check-in dates
- Conditions for extension or removal

### 4.2 Training methods
Use supervised real work, mock scenarios, shadowing, checklists, and review of completed records. Avoid relying only on verbal explanation.

Recommended competencies:
- **Support:** Accurate answers, ticket notes, boundaries, and escalation.
- **Moderation:** Evidence, de-escalation, discipline matrix, and neutral communication.
- **Development:** Branching, testing, review, deployment, rollback, and secret handling.
- **Build/Lore/NPC:** Scope adherence, backups, canon review, performance awareness, and spoiler control.
- **Events/Content:** Runbooks, approvals, public wording, media handling, and contingency planning.

### 4.3 Check-ins
At least weekly during active probation:
1. Review completed work and errors.
2. Give one or two specific improvements.
3. Confirm workload and availability.
4. Update permitted independent actions.
5. Record the check-in briefly.

### 4.4 Mistakes during probation
A good-faith mistake reported promptly is primarily a training issue unless it involves gross negligence, dishonesty, harassment, deliberate rule-breaking, or concealed damage. Restore affected data/access where needed, document the lesson, and update training.

### 4.5 Final review
Choose one outcome:
- **Pass:** Full role within normal permission limits.
- **Extend:** Additional time with specific unmet goals and a new date.
- **Reassign:** Better fit in another role with revised access.
- **End appointment:** Remove access through STF-005 and explain the outcome respectfully.

Do not extend probation indefinitely. A second extension requires Staff Manager review.

## 5. When to get senior help

Immediately pause independent authority if the member shares login details, leaks confidential material, falsifies evidence, retaliates against players, uses tools for personal gain, or creates repeated production risk.

## 6. What to record

- Probation plan
- Check-in notes
- Training scenarios/results
- Permission changes
- Final decision and reasoning

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Goals and boundaries documented
- [ ] Mentor assigned
- [ ] Regular check-ins completed
- [ ] Work samples reviewed
- [ ] Errors addressed and recorded
- [ ] Final outcome approved
- [ ] Access matches final role
