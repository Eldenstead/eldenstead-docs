# STF-004 — Shift Handover and Staff Updates

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Make sure important work and open problems are passed clearly from one staff member to another.

## 2. Scope

All ongoing moderation cases, support tickets, incidents, releases, builds, events, website work, and other tasks that may continue after the current staff member leaves.

## 3. Who does what

- **Current owner:** Leaves a usable handover before stepping away.
- **Receiving owner:** Confirms receipt and asks about missing facts.
- **Team lead:** Resolves unclear ownership and stale work.
- **All staff:** Keep decisions in official channels or records rather than private memory.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Use the correct location
- Player-specific matters: ticket or case record.
- Live incidents: incident channel and report.
- Development/build tasks: issue tracker or approved project channel.
- General staff decisions: relevant staff channel with a link to the authoritative record.
- Login Details and secrets: approved secret manager only, never handover text.

### 4.2 Minimum handover format
Include:
1. **Status:** What is happening now.
2. **Impact:** Players/services affected and severity.
3. **Actions taken:** Commands, punishments, changes, communications, and timestamps.
4. **Evidence:** Links to logs, screenshots, commits, or backups.
5. **Next action:** Exact step needed and who should do it.
6. **Do not do:** Risks, pending approvals, or actions already ruled out.
7. **Owner:** Named person or role accepting the work.

### 4.3 Handover timing
Create a handover before logging off when:
- A case remains unresolved
- A punishment or appeal needs senior review
- A server change is mid-deployment
- A backup/restore or investigation is active
- An event is underway
- A public announcement is expected

### 4.4 Internal communication rules
- Be concise, factual, and respectful.
- Separate confirmed facts from hypotheses.
- Do not ping entire teams for a routine issue.
- Do not move a disputed decision into private DMs to avoid review.
- Summarize verbal or voice decisions in writing.
- Correct inaccurate information openly in the same internal location.

### 4.5 Stale ownership
If the assigned owner is unavailable and delay creates risk, the team lead reassigns the task. Record the reassignment; do not silently take over and erase context.

## 5. When to get senior help

SEV-1/SEV-2 incidents require direct confirmation from the receiving Incident Lead. If no one accepts ownership, escalate to an Administrator or Founder before leaving unless personal safety or an emergency prevents it.

## 6. What to record

- Handover message
- Current owner and receiving owner
- Related ticket/incident/task links
- Pending approvals and deadlines

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Status and impact stated
- [ ] Actions and timestamps recorded
- [ ] Evidence linked
- [ ] Next action is specific
- [ ] Risks and restrictions stated
- [ ] Receiving owner named or escalation completed
