# STF-002 — Welcoming and Setting Up New Staff

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Access is granted only after onboarding requirements are completed, not simply when the Discord role is assigned.

## 1. Purpose

Give new staff the information, access, rules, and support they need to work safely from their first day.

## 2. Scope

All permanent, trial, temporary, paid, and volunteer staff.

## 3. Who does what

- **Staff Manager:** Coordinates onboarding and confirms confirmations.
- **Team lead:** Provides role-specific training and first assignments.
- **Access owner:** Grants approved permissions.
- **New staff member:** Completes security setup, reads required SOPs, and asks before acting outside training.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Create the onboarding record
Record the person's display name, Minecraft UUID/name, Discord account, assigned role, start date, probation end date, team lead, and approved systems.

### 4.2 Required reading and confirmation
Before high-level access is granted, the new staff member must read and confirm:
- Staff conduct and confidentiality
- Role authority and escalation
- Account security and login detail rules
- Relevant moderation, support, development, build, or content SOPs
- No-leak expectations for unannounced content

### 4.3 Security setup
1. Require a unique password and multi-factor authentication on Discord, email, code repository, host, website, and other supported systems.
2. Confirm recovery methods belong to the staff member and are not shared.
3. Do not send passwords or tokens through ordinary Discord messages.
4. Grant individual accounts wherever possible; avoid shared logins.

### 4.4 Setting up access
Give each person only the access needed for their job. A new Moderator does not need host or database access. A Builder does not need billing or punishment access. A Developer can start with code and test-server access. Live-server access needs clear approval.

Maintain an access inventory with:
- Service and role
- Permission level
- Granting approver
- Date granted
- Expiration date for temporary access

### 4.5 Orientation
Cover:
1. Eldenstead's community-first and non-pay-to-win direction.
2. Staff channels and where decisions are recorded.
3. How to identify official announcements and current priorities.
4. How to escalate incidents, player safety concerns, and staff concerns.
5. The difference between normal gameplay, roleplay, testing, and official staff action.
6. Current known limitations and features that remain unannounced.

### 4.6 First assignment
Give a low-risk task that can be reviewed. Examples include answering a supervised support ticket, reviewing a mock report, testing a staging change, completing a small approved build section, or drafting an internal-only post.

### 4.7 Finishing onboarding
The team lead signs off only after the new member shows safe tool use, clear records, respectful communication, and good judgment about when to ask for help. Then begin the trial period in STF-003.

## 5. When to get senior help

Do not give live-server console, billing, database, secret, or owner access just because it is convenient. A Founder must approve any exception, which must have a reason and end date.

## 6. What to record

- Staff onboarding checklist
- Signed/recorded confirmations
- Access inventory
- Assigned mentor or team lead
- First-task review
- Probation dates

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Identity and accounts recorded
- [ ] Required SOPs acknowledged
- [ ] MFA and recovery checked
- [ ] Least-privilege access granted
- [ ] Orientation completed
- [ ] First supervised task completed
- [ ] Probation owner and review date assigned
