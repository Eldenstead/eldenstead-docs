# STF-001 — Hiring and Applicant Checks

> New to staff work? See the [Plain-language glossary](../00_PLAIN_LANGUAGE_GLOSSARY.md) for technical terms.

## 1. Purpose

Choose staff members fairly, safely, and based on the needs of the role.

## 2. Scope

Applications and direct recruitment for Moderator, Helper, Builder, Developer, Administrator, Manager, Event, Lore, and Content roles, whether paid or voluntary.

## 3. Who does what

- **Staff Manager:** Owns the opening, screening, interviews, references, and recommendation.
- **Role lead:** Assesses role-specific skill through an appropriate practical review.
- **Founders:** Approve Administrator, Manager, Developer with production access, paid roles, and exceptions.
- **Interviewers:** Keep applications confidential and avoid promises before approval.

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Open a role
1. Write a concise posting that identifies the role, duties, voluntary or paid status, minimum age if applicable, expected activity, required experience, and application method.
2. Do not promise future payment, promotion, ownership, revenue share, or permanent access unless approved in writing by a Founder.
3. Set an application window or state that recruitment is ongoing.

### 4.2 Initial screening
Review each application for:
- Complete answers and ability to follow instructions
- Relevant examples rather than unsupported claims
- Availability that matches Eldenstead's needs
- Mature communication and willingness to document work
- Conflicts with other projects or communities
- Prior bans, staff removals, or reputation concerns that require explanation

Reject applications that include stolen portfolios, obvious impersonation, threats, harassment, login detail requests, or refusal to follow confidentiality requirements.

### 4.3 Interview
1. Use at least two interviewers for high-access roles.
2. Ask consistent core questions: motivation, conflict handling, availability, examples of mistakes, confidentiality, and escalation judgment.
3. Add role-specific scenarios. Moderators should explain evidence and proportionality; developers should explain testing and rollback; builders should discuss palette, scale, and revision; content staff should explain spoiler control.
4. Record factual notes, not personality insults or protected personal information.

### 4.4 Practical review
- **Moderator/Helper:** Written ticket or moderation scenario.
- **Builder:** Portfolio plus a small test build in an isolated area; never demand substantial free production work.
- **Developer:** Code sample, technical discussion, or small isolated task; do not provide production secrets during testing.
- **Content/Event:** Sample post, event outline, or editing demonstration using non-confidential material.

### 4.5 References and risk checks
For senior or high-level roles, verify prior team experience where practical. Ask the applicant to explain relevant removals or disputes. Do not treat rumors as proof; record sources and allow a response.

### 4.6 Decision
1. Classify as accept, probationary accept, hold, or decline.
2. Document the reason in internal notes.
3. Send a clear outcome without debating confidential scoring.
4. Accepted applicants proceed to STF-002 and receive only the access needed for onboarding.

### 4.7 Reapplication
A normal decline may reapply after 30 days unless the message states otherwise. Dishonesty, harassment, malicious activity, or application fraud may result in a longer or permanent restriction.

## 5. When to get senior help

Any concern involving identity fraud, threats, prior malicious access, stolen work, or pressure for login details must be escalated to a Founder. Paid role terms must be approved before work begins.

## 6. What to record

- Original application
- Interview notes and interviewer names
- Practical review outcome
- Conflict disclosures
- Final decision and date
- Approved compensation terms, if any

## 7. Final checklist

- [ ] Posting accurately identifies paid/voluntary status
- [ ] Application screened consistently
- [ ] Interview completed
- [ ] Role-specific ability reviewed
- [ ] Risks and conflicts documented
- [ ] Approval obtained for high-level or paid role
- [ ] Applicant notified
- [ ] Onboarding initiated only after acceptance
